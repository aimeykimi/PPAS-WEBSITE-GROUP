About Perbadanan Perpustakaan Awam Selangor

Perbadanan Perpustakaan Awam Selangor (PPAS), also known as the Selangor Public Library Corporation, is a prominent state statutory body in Malaysia that has been operational since its establishment in 1971. The corporation was created under the Selangor Public Library Corporation Enactment, which was first gazetted in the Government Gazette No. 5 on March 27, 1969. Its primary mandate is to provide comprehensive public library services and facilities to all citizens of Selangor.

OUR MISSION & VISION

Mission: The mission of PPAS is to provide comprehensive library services that cater to the diverse needs of the community. This includes promoting reading habits, providing access to a wide range of information resources, and fostering lifelong learning among all segments of society. The organization aims to be a center for knowledge dissemination and cultural enrichment.

It aspires to create an informed society that values education, culture, and community engagement. OUR HISTORY

HOW PERBADANAN PERPUSTAKAAN AWAM SELANGOR WAS BEGIN

The Perbadanan Perpustakaan Awam Selangor (PPAS) was established as part of the Malaysian government’s initiative to enhance public library services across the nation. The foundation of PPAS can be traced back to the early 1990s when the state of Selangor recognized the need for a structured library system that could cater to the growing population and their educational needs. The establishment aimed not only to provide access to books and information but also to promote reading culture among the citizens. This initiative was aligned with Malaysia’s broader objectives of fostering knowledge and lifelong learning, which were essential for national development.


In its formative years, PPAS focused on creating a network of libraries throughout Selangor, ensuring that even rural areas had access to library services. The organization began with a few branches and gradually expanded its reach by incorporating modern technologies and diverse resources into its offerings. This included digital libraries, online catalogues, and various community programs designed to engage different demographics within the state. Over time, PPAS has evolved into a comprehensive public library system that not only serves as a repository of knowledge but also as a community hub for cultural and educational activities.
